# Metrics Answer Framework

## Key Terms

- GAME: Clarify, Goals, Actions, Metrics, Evaluate.
- North star metric: The single metric that best captures product or business success.
- KPI: Key Performance Indicator.
- Guardrail metric: A metric that protects against damaging another important outcome.

## 30-Second Answer Structure

I would first clarify scope and assumptions, then define the product goal. From there, I would map the user actions that prove the goal is being achieved, translate those actions into candidate metrics, choose the strongest metric or set of metrics, and evaluate blind spots with guardrail metrics.

## GAME

- Clarify: address ambiguity and scope.
- Goals: define success.
- Actions: identify user behaviors that support the goal.
- Metrics: translate actions into measurable indicators.
- Evaluate: assess weaknesses, blind spots, and guardrails.

## Common Metric Types

- Product metrics: DAU, CTR, time spent.
- OKRs: acquired users, DAU growth, churn reduction.
- Business metrics: revenue, net profit margin, customer lifetime value.
- Quality metrics: bug backlog, MTTR, page load time.
- Leading indicators: signups, button clicks.
- Counter or guardrail metrics: churn rate, customer satisfaction.

## Instagram Discover Example

Goal:

Help users develop and expand their interests.

Chosen metric:

Time spent looking at content by user per session.

Weakness:

Users may spend more time because they are bored or cannot find interesting content.

Guardrail:

Combine time spent with engagement metrics such as likes or creators followed from Discover.

## Pitfalls

- Do not give three metrics if asked for one north star metric.
- Do not use vague concepts like engagement as the metric.
- Define the measurement clearly.
- Match metric granularity to product scope.
- Always discuss blind spots and guardrails.
